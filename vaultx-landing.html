Security model

Real encryption, not a marketing word

Every entry is encrypted before it ever touches storage — here's exactly how.

PBKDF2 key derivation

Your master password runs through 250,000 rounds of PBKDF2 to derive an AES-256 key — never stored, only ever held in memory.

AES-256-GCM at rest

Vault entries are encrypted with authenticated AES-GCM, so even a corrupted or tampered file fails to decrypt rather than silently returning garbage.

Local-only, always

There's no backend. Your encrypted vault lives in your browser's local storage on your device — nothing is ever transmitted anywhere.

Auto-lock on idle

Step away and VaultX locks itself after 5 minutes, clearing the decryption key from memory until you unlock again.

How it works

Three steps, no account required

01

Create a master password

Choose one password to protect everything. It's never sent anywhere — it only ever derives an encryption key in your browser's memory.

02

Generate & save entries

Create strong, random passwords for each site and save them to your vault along with the username and any notes.

03

Unlock whenever you need it

Come back anytime — even after restarting your browser — and unlock with the same master password to decrypt your vault.

Worth knowing before you rely on this

VaultX is a personal, client-side tool — not an audited commercial product. A few honest tradeoffs:

  • There's no password recovery. Forget your master password and the vault can't be decrypted — by you or anyone else.
  • It's single-device. Your vault doesn't sync across browsers or computers — use Export for backups.
  • For anything beyond casual personal use, an established, audited password manager is the safer choice.
